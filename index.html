01 / About

We exist to close
the AI divide.

AI Britain CIC was founded on a straightforward conviction: the benefits of artificial intelligence must not be reserved for those who can afford them.

We provide free, practical AI education and tools to people who are unemployed, in low-income households, or living in digitally underserved communities. Our focus is on young people — the generation that will either benefit from or be displaced by AI, depending largely on the opportunities available to them now.

As a Community Interest Company, every penny of surplus is locked to our social purpose. We do not exist to extract value from communities. We exist to build it there.

03 / Carnegie Project

Carnegie: Moral Philosophy
& Values in the Digital Age

In 1892, Scottish-American industrialist Andrew Carnegie built a public library on Rosemount Viaduct, Aberdeen — free to all, regardless of class or income. His conviction was that access to knowledge was the foundation of human advancement.

Next door stands St Mark's Church — Carnegie's architectural neighbour — now vacant for five years and listed for sale by the Church of Scotland. AI Britain CIC proposes to acquire and convert this building into Scotland's first dedicated community AI innovation centre for young people, named in honour of Carnegie's philosophy.

"The library next door gave free access to books in 1892. The Carnegie Centre will give free access to artificial intelligence in the same spirit, in the same street, 133 years later."
AI Britain CIC, Proposal to the General Trustees, 2025

The centre will serve young Scots from low-income and unemployed backgrounds — providing AI workstations, a dedicated content creation studio, entrepreneurship programmes, and research facilities. All services will be free at the point of use.

We are seeking a partnership with the Church of Scotland that reflects the shared values embedded in this building's history: community service, access to knowledge, and investment in the next generation.
